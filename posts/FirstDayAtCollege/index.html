My First Day at College

Feb 8 2021-02-08T21:30:00-03:00 by Eduardo Adame
Updated Jun 15 2021-06-15T18:33:16-03:00 4 min

What were my expectations about today?

Well, yesterday I was really anxious about today. I thought I’d start the day with a Analytical Geometry class, at 7:30am. And, at 8:30am, I’d go to the school year beginning ceremony and there I’d meet all my classmates and teachers. When it was over, the next classes would be prepared for me (and my new classmates), and so I’d study Calculus and Computing.

At 1pm, I’d a have a good lunch and, at 2pm, go to the meeting with the coordination of my scholarship, and they would tell us what they expect we to do during the course.

What I started to study.

I’ve joined the Bachelor of Data Science at Getulio Vargas Foundation (Fundação Getúlio Vargas/FGV), specifically at Applied Mathematics School (Escola de Matemática Aplicada/EMAp). I decided to do so because I really wanted to use my ease of learn math in realsituations. So, why not Applied Mathematics course? Because I think I have a real connection with Computing in general, and I am almost sure I’m hungry of math because of this. I hope I make some articles here about Data Science.

As I won olympic medals in the past, I could be part of a scholarship program there in FGV. So, I don’t pay anything to them and, when I go to Rio de Janeiro, I’ll have a hotel to live and a monetary aid.

What really happened?

The first hours.

I woke up at 5am, without any alarm, even wanting to do this only at 6:30am. As I’ve said later, I was really anxious. Alright, I took a cold shower and went kitchen to eat something. But, when I got there, I was so seasick that I couldn’t eat anything. It happens. So, at 7:30am I was sit in front of my PC waiting for the teatcher to start the class.

I was not alone, my classmates (we already had a whatsapp group) were waiting too. When it passed 15 minutes, we noticed he wouldn’t come. As the ceremony only would start at 8:30am, we thought the school year would start there too. And then we created a google meet video conference and started to play GarticPhone together. I was really fun, I encourage you to play this with your friends.

We had a good time, but the clock ticked 8:30am…

The classes.

We entered in the ceremony at the right hour, we listened to the principal and the coordinators (one for each course) and started to watch the same presentation we had other 2 times - one before being joined, and other right after. But that’s ok, some few people didn’t watch other times and they could want to. And, obviously, it was my first day and I was really happy and excited. But, something happend.

Our calculus monitor - a brilliant guy - said to us that the calculus class was going on. The teacher didn’t matter to teach while the ceremony, I thought. There was 6 (from 80) people there in the class. But I decided to go watch her. I told to my mates, and they started to come in too.

She looks like a good teacher, she was very kind when talking to us. After the class, I started to think she didn’t know about the ceremony, once her is from other FGV School. During the class, she mainly introduced her course.

The morning end.

Before the Calculus class, we went to the Computing one. But, our teacher didn’t appear (again), and we think it’s because it was the first day, some hour later he explained when would be the first class. In stead of getting out of the room, we started to talk about reality shows and our local culture. I told about Angra dos Reis, others told about Brasília, Rio de Janeiro, south states, etc.

The after lunch.

At 2pm, we - only the scholarship students - went to a meeting with our coordination, lead by DSc. Camacho. He congratulated us by joining in College with a scholarship. He is also the creator of the olympiad that I’m a medalist. He was kind of a unreachble man for me, meeting him realized my day. I had some talking with him, he joked, told stories, motivated all us. I’m pretty glad to join his team.

That’s it, it wasn’t how I expected, but was the best I could’ve had.
